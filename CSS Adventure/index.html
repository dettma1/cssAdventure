The First Computer Programmer

Ada Lovelace was born in the 1800s. In her mid 30s she worked with Charles Babbage, known as the father of computing. She wrote an algorithm designed to be carried out by the machine Babbage was working on. Her manuscript is titled "Notes" and is regarded as the first computer program, making Lovelace the first computer programmer.

Grace Hopper

Grace Hopper is famous for coining the term "bug" (when code doesn't run correctly) when she found a moth in her own computer. Hopper was working in the Navy on computing when she suggested a new programming language be invented that used entirely engligh words. She was told computers couldn't understand english and the idea was absurd. Hopper then invented the first compiler, which takes human readable code and translates it into machine readable code.

The First Computers

Before the computer we know existed, women who excelled in math and science were hired to compute mathmatical formulas. Katherine Johnson, Dorothy Vaughan, and Mary Jackson were all women who worked on the Apallo Mission. Their stories were made famous by the book and movie Hidden Figures. All three women worked extremly hard for their place in their field and were often denied recogniziation for their work because of their gender and race.
